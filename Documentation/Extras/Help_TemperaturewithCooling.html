Temperature with Cooling

Block Inputs

Ploss - Power Loss Output from Electrical Component, W
Tamb - Ambient Temperature; Temperature at Sea-Level Static Conditions for a Standard Day, °F
Tcool - Temperature of Coolant, °F
Temp(t-1) - Previous temperature output delayed 1 timestep, °F
Q - Integrated component internal heat (Qdot), J

Block Outputs

Temp - Temperature of the Electrical Component, °F
Temp(t) - Current temperature output to be delayed one time step, °F
Qdot - Change in heat energy of the Electrical Component, W

Block Mask

m - Mass of the Electrical Component, lbm
c_p - Specific Heat of the Electrical Component, Btu/(lbm-°F)
COP = (h*A_s) - Product of Coefficient of Convective Heat Transfer and Surface Area; Coefficient of Performance, Btu/(hr-°F)
(Note initial heat should be specified in an external integrator and not block mask)
Q_o - Initial Heat of the Electrical Component, Btu

Block Calculations

Q ̇ = mc_p (ΔTemp)
Q ̇_convection = hA_s (Temp-Tin_Cool)
Q ̇ = Ploss-Q ̇_convection
Temp = int_0^t [Ploss-COP(Temp-Tin_Cool)] m^(-1) c_p^(-1) dt
Note:
For an Aerospace systems moving at Mach Number > 0.3, Tamb can be calculated as T_static via:
T_t = T_s * [1 + M^2*(gamma-1)/2]
where gamma is the ratio of specific heats (c_p/c_v) in air.
